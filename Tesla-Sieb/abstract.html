The Barbelo-Tesla Prime Sieve:
A Visual Framework for Prime Distribution
via Iterative Digit-Sum Reduction

Abstract

This paper presents the Barbelo-Tesla Prime Sieve, a tabular visualization of prime number distribution based on iterative digit-sum reduction. All natural numbers are classified by their digital root (iterative digit sum) into nine classes (1–9). Classes 3, 6, and 9 are structurally excluded: every member of these classes is divisible by 3 and therefore cannot be prime (with the single exception of 3 itself).

This leaves six active classes — digital roots 1, 2, 4, 5, 7, and 8 — in which all primes greater than 3 must appear. When prime numbers are entered into a table organized by these six classes, a recurring structural pattern becomes visible:

6 candidate positions  ·  1 certain gap (DS 9)  ·  2 candidate positions  ·  1 certain gap (DS 3)  ·  repeating

The three structurally empty classes (DS 3, DS 6, DS 9) act as fixed dividers within this repeating 10-position cycle. Beyond these structural gaps, additional empty positions arise from multiples of primes themselves — the classical sieving process — which are displayed as a secondary overlay.

Method

The sieve is constructed as follows:

  • Compute the iterative digit sum (digital root) of each natural number.
  • Arrange numbers in a table where each column corresponds to one digital-root class.
  • Columns DS 3, DS 6, DS 9 remain structurally empty (except the prime 3 in column DS 3).
  • Mark primes in the remaining six columns; mark composite members of those columns as sieved.

The result is a visual sieve that separates structural impossibility (digit-sum constraint) from empirical sparsity (composite numbers). The 6+1+2+1 rhythm of candidate and gap positions repeats indefinitely and is invariant across all number ranges.

Implementation

The visualization is implemented as a pure HTML5 interactive table with no external dependencies. An adjustable range slider controls how many numbers are displayed (up to 1,000). A toggle allows composites to be shown or hidden. Hovering over any cell reveals the full digit-sum chain for that number.

Relation to Prior Work

This visualization is part of a series of prime number representations by the same author. The Barbelo Prime Tree I (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20475174) and Barbelo Prime Tree II (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20497826) organize primes as branching tree structures based on prime gaps and the OEIS sequence A333489 respectively. The Barbelo Prime Sieve approaches distribution from a digit-theoretic perspective, complementing the geometric tree representations.
